
The current job market is brutal.
Unfortunately, AI engineers are training for roles that won’t exist.

Andrew Ng and Laurence Moroney gave the most honest career advice I've seen.

Companies overhired in 2022-2023. 
Now they only want production skills.

Here's are 5 things that actually matter:
🔸 Collapse the stack 
↳ AI tools made coding faster and cheaper 
↳ The bottleneck shifted to product thinking 
↳ Best engineers write code AND shape specs 
↳ Waiting for specs to execute is a shrinking role

🔸 Choose teams, not brands 
↳ Hot logos attract everyone 
↳ You end up wherever they need bodies 
↳ Your colleagues are your learning environment 
↳ Always ask: "Which exact team will I join?"

🔸 Three pillars that get you hired 
↳ Depth beyond high-level APIs 
↳ Business focus over cool projects 
↳ Bias towards delivery, not ideas

🔸 Manage AI-generated code as debt 
↳ Vibe coding is fast but fragile 
↳ Edge cases, testing, structure get skipped 
↳ Your value: verify, debug, make it production-grade 
↳ "A good coder manages technical debt to get rich"

🔸 Small AI is the opportunity 
↳ Big hosted models are crowded and possibly in a bubble 
↳ Self-hosted, on-device, private is underserved 
↳ Industries with IP concerns can't use ChatGPT 
↳ Fine-tuning small models is tomorrow's high-demand skill

The market correction separates builders from tourists.
Every correction rewards the same profile. 
Engineers who create business value, not just cool demos.
